# Q3
- __Microarrays approach:__
    - (+) suitable for studying quantitative variants
    - (+) genome wide coverage
    - (+) realtively cost effective
    - (-) Low resolution for small CNVs (compared to e.g. Sanger Sequencing)
    - (-) can not detect balanced variants (e.g. inversions)
- __NGS (sequecning-based) approach:__  
    - (+) high resolution (can detect small CNVs or sequence level breakpoint accuracy)
    - (-) computationally costly
    - (-) biased towards detection of deletions
 
- (-) no method curretly can capture the entire structual variation within the genome

# Q4
- 2 criteria for cluster filtering:
    1. Reciprocal overlap between CNVs
    2. Support from multiple independent studies/samples
- The filtering was done to remove false positives (as CNVs that appear in a large number of samples are not likely to be a false positive) and artifacts,  (as the filetr could exclude study-specific artefacts absed on number of studies), to ensure robust, reproducible CNV regions.
