## Genome Resources


UCSC genome browser 
(genome.ucsc.edu)
- originated from the human genome project
- most widely used general genome browser
- many default tracks
- many species
- customization with "BED" files


NCBI
- entry point for genome reference data
- human genome assemblies
- human variants collections:
    - dbSNP (indels,microsatellites,non-polymorphic variants)
    - dbVAR (structural variation: indels, inversions, duplications, substitutions, mobile elements, chr rearrangements)
    - ClinVar (more for the clinics: aggregates genomic information and its relationship to human health)


Other databases for variant resources:
- EMBL-EBI:EVA (not only human data)
- Ensembl


VICC
- standardize curation, representation and  interpretation of clinically relevant evidence associated with genomic variation in cancers
- more "post-organizational"


Other genome resources:
- ALLIANCE of genome resources (for exploration of model organism and human comparative genomics)
    - develop and maintain a knowledge commons platform for comparative genomics that facilitates the use of diverse model organisms to investigate the genetic and genomic basis of human biology, health, and disease
    - example: Multiple Genome Viewer (MGV) - compare genome features of several mouse strains as well as human, fly, frog, rat, worm, yeast, and zebrafish
- NCGR (National Center for Genome Resources)
    - Pangenomic algorithm development and application
    - Genome assembly and scaffolding
    - Short read, long read, or hybrid sequence data
    - BioNano, HiC, or genetic map data
    - Whole genome, reduces representation, or targeted data
    - SNP and small indel discovery
    - Structural variant discovery
    - Epigenetics and ChIP-seq analysis
- GenomeNet (Japanese network of database and computational services for genome research and related research areas in biomedical sciences)
