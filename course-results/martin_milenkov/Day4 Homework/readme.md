## Human HBA and HBB
Using [**emboss-stretcher**](https://www.ebi.ac.uk/jdispatcher/psa/emboss_stretcher) with parameters: Matrix: EBLOSUM62, Gap_penalty: 12 and Extend_penalty: 2, alignment between the alpha and beta chain of human hemaglobin (**HBA** and **HBB**) showed that they are moderately similar in sequence ([score of 277, similarity 60%, identity 44% gaps 6.0%](./Human%20alignments/HBA%20and%20HBB%20protein%20alignment.out)), with many amino acids despite not aligning perfectly between both proteins, still having similar properties. Running **BLAST** with the protein sequence for **HBA1** has **HBA2** showing up with [98% identity and a total score of 2527 or bits score 1368](/Human%20alignments/BLAST%20HBA1%20HBA2%20alignment.txt).

On a genetic level, again using stretcher with parameters Matrix: EDNAFULL, Gap_penalty: 16, and Extend_penalty: 4, the genes for **HBA1** and **HBA2** are extremely similar ([identity and similarity 83%, gaps 4% and score of 6832](./Human%20alignments/HBA1%20vs%20HBA2.out)) with nearly perfectly preserved sequence in the first half of each gene (stretch 1 - 1050) and only moderately similar towards the latter half (stretch 1051-2035). Most likely, either gene has appeared as a result of duplication of the other.

Comparing one of the **HBA** genes with that of **HBB** shows much less similarity - both in length and makeup, with only sporadically aligned nucleotides ([identity and similarity of 27%, gaps 60% and score of -10'749](./Human%20alignments/HB1%20vs%20HBB%20gene.out)). This makes it likely that the genes for both subunits have evolved separately and the proteins have converged structurally, and neither gene has arisen from duplication of the other.

## Mouse HBA and HBB
Looking at the protein alignment between adult alpha chain (**Hba**) and adult beta chain (**Hbb-b1**) shows that they are somewhat similar in length and functional groups ([identity 42%, similarity 58%, gaps 6%, score 251](./Mouse%20alignments/Emboss%20stretcher%20mouse%20Hba%20and%20Hbb-b1%20protein%20alignment.out)) with large amount of amino acids that differ in identity but being of similar chemical character, just like in human HBA and HBB.\
Comparison of the genes for one of the alpha chains, for example **Hba-a1**, and that for the adult beta chain **Hbb-bs**, reveals some similarity between them ([similarity and identity 45%, gaps 27%, score -1796](./Mouse%20alignments/Emboss%20stretcher%20mouse%20hba-a1%20and%20hbb-b1%20gene%20alignment.out)), but the genes are different enough to doubt either appearing as a result of duplication of the other. Interestingly, the similarity between both appears significantly higher than in the human homologs.

Looking at the genes encoding the protein myoglobin subunits, in the case of the alpha subunit the **Hba-a1** and **Hba-a2** genes, similarly to humans, show a great amount of conservation ([identity and similarity at 87%, gaps at 9% and score of 8274](./Mouse%20alignments/Emboss%20stretcher%20mouse%20Hba-a1%20and%20Hba-a2%20genes.out)) with mostly identical bases within the middle of the alignment (approximately positions 151-2000 on Hba-a1 gene in alignment), with 2 instances of deleted or duplicated fragments about 10 nucleotides in length, and more noticable discrepancy towards either end at the alignment.

The gene for adult myoglobin beta chain, **Hbb-bs**, can also be compared to the embryonic gene **Hbb-y** by aligning them. The alignment reveals that although both genes code for functionally nearly identical protein, the similarity between both genes is only moderate ([identity and similarity at 50%, gaps 15%, and score of 191](./Mouse%20alignments/Emboss%20stretcher%20mouse%20Hbb-y%20and%20Hbb-bs%20gene%20alignment.out)).\
Comparing the protein sequences for the products of both genes: Hbb-b1 and Hbb-y (with [similarity 71%, identity 84.4%, gaps 0%](./Mouse%20alignments/Emboss%20stretcher%20mouse%20Hbb-b1%20and%20Hbb-y%20protein%20alignment.out)) further shows that even though both proteins have the same role and conserved functional similarity of amino acids residues, they differ across both. Still, the similarity is considerably higher than between the Hba and Hbb-b1 chains.
