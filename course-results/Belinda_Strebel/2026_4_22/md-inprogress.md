# Uniprot exercise
- Uniprot ID: B1P0S1
- Protein Name: Antifreete protein (Maxi type AFP)
- Length: 195-200
- Keywords: Antifreeze, Ice-binding, secreted protein, cold adaption, extracellular protein
- function: binds to ice crystals and prevents their growth, lowers freezing point of body fluids (thermal hysteresis), protects organisms in subzero environemtents
- domains: Ligand-binding: binds ice, often contains ice-binding surface motifs
- gene ontology
  molecular functions: ice binding, structural molecule activity
  biological process: response to cold, inhibition of ice crystal growth
  cellular components: extracellular region/ blood plasma 
- which mutations and diseases: no human disease associated, large evolutionary variation
- sub-cellular location secreted and extracellular
- which PTM: depends on type: Disulfide bonds, Glycosylation, Signal peptide cleavage
- are there isoforms: yes, very common: multipe types, and there are multiple within species through gene duplications.
  the different isoforms have different ice binding strengths 
- Orthologs/ Paralogs
  Orthologs: found across fish, insects, plants, fungi and bacteria
  Paralogs: extensive gene duplication in fish
  important note: AFPs evolved multiple times independently (convergent evolution)
